## Strategic Intelligence: Kharkiv & Kupiansk Operations

### Kharkiv Assault (July 2025) — First Fully Unmanned Victory
**Command:** Junior Lieutenant Mykola "Makar" Zinkevsky (NC13 Robotic Strike Unit, 3rd Separate Assault Brigade)

**Operation Details:**
- 30 kg explosive robots deployed across valley toward Russian trench
- Aerial drone provided close air support (bomb drop to clear path)
- Two Russian soldiers surrendered and were captured
- **First combat operation in history where a position fell to unmanned platforms alone**
- Zero Ukrainian infantry casualties (no personnel entered fight)

**Strategic Significance:** Zelensky used this operation in promotional video to European leaders (April 13, 2026) to pitch unmanned warfare doctrine export.

### Kupiansk School Operation (February 2026) — Advanced Tactics
**Command:** Major Andrii Kopach (Unmanned Ground Systems Company, Khartia Corps National Guard)

**Tactical Complexity:**
- Target: Russian-occupied thick-walled school with window netting (defeats aerial drones)
- Solution: Nighttime multi-robot assault during snowstorm (Russian drone coverage cut)
- Operators: Remote control from city far from frontlines using radio/video relays
- Robot 1: Thermobaric rocket launcher (opens fire, forces soldiers from windows)
- Robot 2: 227 kg+ explosive payload (enters building)
- Robot 3: Secondary explosive platform
- Result: Building collapsed, 9+ Russian soldiers neutralized, ammunition depot destroyed, 1 escaped soldier eliminated by accompanying aerial drone

**Tactical Lesson:** Hardware is simple; tactics and training determine effectiveness.

## Risk Factors & Challenges

### Technical Challenges
1. **Battery Life:** Most systems last 24 hours before battery death or Russian fire
2. **Communication Reliability:** Primary development focus
3. **Off-Road Capability:** Secondary development focus
4. **Thermal Signature:** Partially addressed (BIZON-L liquid cooling)

### Operational Challenges
1. **Robot Visibility:** Slower/more visible than aerial drones
2. **Position Holding:** Requires personnel to hold position after robot clears
3. **Operator Fatigue:** Extended remote control operations
4. **Training Curve:** Sophisticated tactics required for effectiveness

### Strategic Challenges
1. **Production Scaling:** 25,000 unit H1 2026 target (capacity test)
2. **Component Supply:** Foreign dependency on 15-30% of systems
